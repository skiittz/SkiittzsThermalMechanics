================================================================================
           SKIITTZ'S THERMAL MECHANICS - Player Guide
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This mod adds a heat management system to Space Engineers. Your power-producing
blocks (reactors, batteries, and hydrogen engines) and hydrogen thrusters now
generate heat while operating. If heat builds up too much, blocks will take
damage and can be destroyed. You'll need to build heat sinks and radiators to
keep your grid cool.


THE HEAT CYCLE - HOW IT WORKS
-----------------------------
1. Power blocks and H2 thrusters generate heat based on their current output.
2. Each block has a small amount of passive cooling built in, but it is not
   enough on its own under load.
3. Excess heat flows into your grid's Heat Sink (a special beacon block).
4. Radiators pull heat out of the heat sink and vent it into the environment.
5. If heat is not removed fast enough, blocks overheat and take damage.


KEY BLOCKS
----------

  HEAT SOURCES (blocks that produce heat):
  
    Reactors        - Generate heat proportional to their power output (MW).
    Batteries       - Generate heat when discharging.
    Hydrogen Engines- Generate heat proportional to their power output.
    Hydrogen Thrusters - Generate heat based on current thrust output.

  HEAT MANAGEMENT (blocks that handle heat):

    Heat Sink       - A special beacon block that acts as a central heat
                      reservoir for your entire grid. All heat from generators
                      and thrusters flows into the heat sink. You only need ONE
                      active heat sink per grid (extras are automatically
                      disabled). 

    Radiators       - Blocks that pull heat out of the heat sink and
                      vent it away. They ramp up and down gradually. You can
                      (and should) have multiple radiators. They come in Basic
                      and Advanced tiers, and in both DLC and non-DLC variants.

      IMPORTANT: Radiators MUST have a clear line of sight to the sky to
      function. If they are buried inside your ship or blocked by armor, they
      will not dissipate any heat. Build them on the exterior of your grid.


OVERHEATING
-----------
When a power block's heat reaches 100% of its capacity:
  - It takes damage every tick it stays overheated.
  - Each overheat cycle increases "Thermal Fatigue," a permanent penalty that
    makes the block generate even more heat in the future.
  - If damaged enough, the block will become non-functional and shut down.
  - You will receive in-game chat warnings as heat builds up.

Thermal Fatigue is permanent and cannot be repaired. Take care of your blocks!


HEAT SINK DESTRUCTION & SEPARATION
-----------------------------------
If your heat sink is destroyed or separated from the grid (e.g., by grinding
or battle damage), all stored heat is fed back into the power blocks on the
original grid. This can cause a cascading overheat event, so protect your heat
sink!

If destroyed, the heat sink will try to transfer its stored heat to any
remaining heat sinks on the grid before feeding it back to generators.


SMALL GRID TO LARGE GRID HEAT SHUNTING
--------------------------------------
Small grid heat sinks can shunt their stored heat to a connected large grid
heat sink through connectors. If your small grid ship is docked to a large
grid station via connectors, the small grid's heat will automatically transfer
to the large grid's heat sink for dissipation.


IR SIGNAL (DETECTION RANGE)
----------------------------
As your radiators vent heat, the heat sink's beacon range increases,
representing an infrared signature. The more heat you vent, the easier it is
for other players to detect your grid on their HUD. The signal decays over
time when heat output decreases. In PvP scenarios, managing your heat output
can help you stay hidden.


WEATHER EFFECTS
---------------
Weather on planets affects how well your radiators work AND your IR signal:

  Weather              Dissipation   Signal Range
  ---------------------------------------------------
  Space                  Normal        Normal
  Rain (Light)           +11%          Normal
  Rain (Heavy)           +25%          +15%
  Thunderstorm (Light)   +25%          Normal
  Thunderstorm (Heavy)   +43%          Normal
  Snow (Light)           +25%          Normal
  Snow (Heavy)           +43%          Normal
  Fog (Light)            -25%          -25%
  Fog (Heavy)            -50%          -50%
  Sandstorm (Light)      -25%          -25%
  Sandstorm (Heavy)      -50%          -50%
  Electric Storm         -50%          Normal

Rain and storms help cool your ship; fog and sandstorms make cooling harder.


COCKPIT HUD
------------
When you sit in a cockpit, a HUD notification displays your grid's current
heat sink capacity percentage and detection range. This gives you at-a-glance
thermal status without needing to check individual blocks.

You can toggle this HUD on or off with a chat command (see below).


READING BLOCK INFO
------------------
Click on any heat-producing block or heat management block in the terminal to
see thermal stats in the block's custom info panel:

  Power Blocks show:
    - Heat Level (percentage of capacity)
    - Time until overheat or fully cooled (hh:mm:ss countdown)
    - Thermal Fatigue (permanent heat penalty from past overheats)

  Heat Sinks show:
    - Heat Level (percentage of capacity)
    - Current IR Detectable Distance (meters)

  Radiators show:
    - Current dissipation rate (MW and percentage of max)
    - Warning if the radiator is not exterior-facing


GENERATOR SPAM PENALTY
-----------------------
Using a large number of power generators on one grid incurs a heat penalty.
It is more efficient to use fewer, more powerful generators than to spam many
small ones.


CHAT BOT ASSISTANT
------------------
The mod includes a chat bot assistant that warns you about thermal issues.
It sends messages when:
  - You have no heat sink on a grid
  - Your heat sink is reaching capacity (50%, 80%, 100%)
  - A block is taking overheat damage
  - A block has been disabled by heat damage
  - A heat sink has been separated from the grid

You can control the assistant with chat commands. The default name is shown
when the assistant first introduces itself. Commands are typed as:
  /<assistant_name> <command>

Available commands:
  help              - Show available commands
  stfu              - Turn off ALL messages
  speak             - Turn messages back on
  iamnotanewb       - Turn off tutorial/tip messages (warnings still show)
  spankemedaddy_iamnewb - Turn tutorial messages back on
  rename <newname>  - Rename the assistant (it will respond to the new name)
  togglehud         - Toggle the cockpit heat HUD on/off
  reload            - (Admin only) Reload configuration from file
  toggledebug       - (Admin only) Toggle debug info display


RADIATOR COLOR CUSTOMIZATION
------------------------------
Radiators have two color pickers in their terminal panel:
  - Idle Color: The color the radiator displays when not dissipating heat.
  - Active Color: The color displayed at peak operation.
The radiator smoothly transitions between these colors based on its current
workload, giving you a visual indicator of thermal activity.


CONFIGURATION (Server Admins)
------------------------------
All settings are stored in a Settings.xml file in the world save folder.
Server admins can customize:
  - Heat capacity and passive cooling for every supported block type
  - Radiator max dissipation and ramp-up speed
  - Heat sink capacity and signal decay rate
  - Weather effect multipliers for dissipation and signal range
  - Chat bot behavior (name, message delay)

After editing the config file, use the "/<assistant_name> reload" chat command
to apply changes without restarting the server.


TIPS FOR NEW PLAYERS
---------------------
1. Always build at least one heat sink on every grid that has power blocks.
2. Place radiators on the outside of your ship where they can see the sky.
3. More radiators = better cooling. Scale them with your power output.
4. Watch the cockpit HUD or check block info to monitor heat levels.
5. In combat, protect your heat sink - losing it can destroy your generators.
6. Use fewer large generators instead of many small ones to avoid spam penalty.
7. On planets, take advantage of rain and storms for bonus cooling.
8. In PvP, be mindful of your IR signature - high heat output makes you
   visible from farther away.
